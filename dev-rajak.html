The Man

About Dev Rajak

"I am not here to fit in. I never was."

Dev Rajak is an Automobile Engineer and Production Assistant working in a private automobile company, with years of hands-on industry experience. A man who believes in building his life on his own terms.

Born and raised in the steel city of Jamshedpur, Jharkhand, Dev carries the grit of the land he comes from. His journey spans technical trade training at Tata Motors, formal engineering education, and a continuing pursuit of knowledge through sociology and human psychology.

Beyond work, Dev is a quiet observer of life — deeply influenced by the philosophy of Osho and the teachings of Buddha. He draws, sketches, travels, and reflects — always on his own terms, always with full ownership of who he is.

Life & Thoughts

Philosophy & Life Chapters

01
The Road Less Travelled
While most walked the conventional path, Dev chose to earn real-world experience at Tata Motors while still young — a bold decision that shaped his technical identity and work ethic before any degree could.
02
The Engineer & The Philosopher
A rare combination — a man who operates machines by day and reads Osho by night. Dev found in philosophy what engineering could not give: the language for the restlessness inside him.
03
Two Brothers, Two Paths
Dev's younger brother Nitish became a Metallurgical Engineer at a multinational firm. Two brothers, different trades, same relentless drive. The Rajak name carries weight in the engineering world.
04
The Art of Letting Go
Influenced deeply by Buddha's teachings, Dev learned early that attachments — to people, to outcomes, to validation — are the root of suffering. He walks lighter because of it.
05
Drawing the World Within
When words aren't enough, Dev picks up a pencil. Drawing and sketching are not just hobbies — they are his way of processing the world, capturing what the eye sees and the mind feels.
06
Trust No One But Yourself
Dev's philosophy is clear and unapologetic: "I trust no single person — and I never will." Not out of bitterness, but out of a hard-won wisdom that self-reliance is the only reliable foundation.
